## FAQ Content

### Q1: What is subsea cable engineering?
**A:** Subsea cable engineering involves the design, planning, and installation of cables that are laid on the ocean floor for power transmission, telecommunications, or data transfer. It requires specialized expertise in marine environments, regulatory compliance, and offshore operations.

### Q2: What types of cables do you work with?
**A:** We work with various cable types including high-voltage AC (HVAC) cables, high-voltage DC (HVDC) cables, fiber optic communication cables, and hybrid power-communication cables. Our expertise covers voltage ratings from 33 kV to 765 kV AC and ±100 kV to ±800 kV DC.

### Q3: What water depths can you handle?
**A:** Our team has experience in all water depths, from shallow water (0-100m) to ultra-deep water (>2000m). We have successfully completed projects in diverse geographic regions including the North Sea, Mediterranean, Atlantic, and Pacific.

### Q4: How long does a typical subsea cable project take?
**A:** Project timelines vary significantly based on cable length, water depth, and complexity. Feasibility studies typically take 2-4 months, detailed design 3-6 months, and installation 2-12 months depending on weather windows and logistics.

### Q5: What regulatory certifications do you hold?
**A:** We comply with international standards including IEC 60502 for power cables, IEC 60794 for optical fiber cables, DNV GL, ABS, and Lloyd's Register standards. We maintain ISO 9001 (Quality), ISO 14001 (Environment), and ISO 45001 (Safety) certifications.

### Q6: How do you assess environmental impact?
**A:** We conduct comprehensive environmental impact assessments including marine ecosystem analysis, sediment disturbance evaluation, and compliance with MARPOL and UNCLOS regulations. We develop mitigation measures to minimize environmental disruption.

### Q7: What is your experience with HVDC cables?
**A:** Our team has extensive experience with HVDC cable systems for long-distance power transmission. We've worked on projects ranging from ±100 kV to ±800 kV DC systems, including both monopole and bipole configurations.

### Q8: Do you provide installation supervision?
**A:** Yes, we provide comprehensive installation supervision including on-site engineering support, quality assurance, testing, commissioning, and final documentation. Our team ensures compliance with design specifications and safety protocols.

### Q9: Can you handle complex cable routes?
**A:** Yes, we specialize in optimizing complex cable routes considering bathymetry, environmental factors, regulatory requirements, and cost considerations. We use advanced analysis tools and have experience in challenging geographic regions.

### Q10: What support do you provide after installation?
**A:** We provide post-installation support including commissioning supervision, performance testing, documentation, and ongoing consultancy for maintenance and optimization of subsea cable systems.
